Worksheet: Modeling Real-Life Situations Using Functions

Grade Level: Grade 11

Topic: Accurately Constructing Mathematical Models Using Functions

Learning Objective: Construct and interpret functions that model real-world scenarios.

🔹 Part A: Guided Examples

Example 1 – Load Usage (Linear Function)

📱 Jane loads ₱100 on her phone. Each call costs ₱5.

Let x be the number of calls, and y be the remaining load.

  • a. Write the function: y = 100 - 5x
  • b. How many calls can she make before her load runs out? x = 20

Example 2 – Weekly Savings (Linear Function)

💰 Luis saves ₱150 per week.

Let x be the number of weeks, and S(x) the total savings.

  • a. Write a function: S(x) = 150x
  • b. Savings after 8 weeks: ₱1,200

🔹 Part B: Practice Problems

1. Movie Tickets (Linear Function) 🎬

  • a. Write a function T(x) for the total cost of x tickets.
  • b. How much will 5 tickets cost?

2. Falling Object (Quadratic Function) 🍎

h(t) = -4.9t² + 20

  • a. How high is the apple after 1 second?
  • b. When will the apple hit the ground?

3. Bacteria Growth (Exponential Function) 🧫

  • a. Write a function P(t) for population after t hours.
  • b. What is the population after 4 hours?

4. Cellphone Value (Exponential Decay) 📱

  • a. Write a function V(t) for value after t years.
  • b. What will be its value after 2 years?

5. Tuition Fee Increase (Linear Function) 🎓

  • a. Write a function F(x) for the tuition fee x years from now.
  • b. What will the fee be in 3 years?

🔹 Part C: Challenge Problem

Water Tank (Piecewise Function) 🚰

  • - First 2 minutes: 10 L/min
  • - Next 3 minutes: 5 L/min
  • - After 5 minutes: Stops filling

a. Define a piecewise function W(t) for total water in the tank.

b. How much water is in the tank after 5 minutes?
